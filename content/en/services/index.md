## How I work

I operate as a **technical working unit** of great autonomy, delivering high-quality outputs for addressing environmental problems. My service system is structured around three core values: (1) **well-defined scope**, (2) **predictable workflow**, and; (3) **standalone outputs**. Clients and collaborators can expect minimal coordination overhead.

## Categories of services

I work across a set of service categories, each defined by a specific level of engagement and workflow.

### 1. Standard methods

**Deliverables for well-defined problems**  
Application of established methods in the environmental field. Outputs include formal technical statements and reports (signed documents), as well as standard analytical or visual products.

**Linear workflow**. A direct process: kickoff for scope definition, a first viable version for validation, and a final refined delivery. Interaction during production is minimal. Outputs are standalone, with full technical documentation and assigned responsibility.

---

### 2. Development support

**Tailored solutions for challenging problems**  
Work in this category involves the creative work, modeling approaches, automated workflows, and visualization systems adapted to the project context.

**Iterative workflow**. The process unfolds through initial framing and structured execution with intermediate check-ins. A longer refinement cycle is expected. Outputs are delivered as versioned and documented instances.

---

### 3. Strategic advisory

**Technical guidance for complex projects**  
Focused on advising ongoing work. The objective is not execution, but improving the quality and consistency of technical decisions. Short written memos may complement discussions when needed.

**Event-based workflow**. Structured around scheduled sessions—such as meetings or workshops—focused on reviewing methods, evaluating assumptions, and contributing to project direction.

---

### 4. Lectures and training

**Shared knowledge and experience**  
Lectures on hydrology and environmental planning, as well as training in hydrological modeling, environmental analysis, and core research tools such as Python and $\LaTeX$ for workflow automation.

---

# Communication and setting

The value of my work depends on the ability to sustain **deep work** (uninterrupted technical focus).

Communication is therefore minimal _by design_. A single channel—typically email—is used to define scope, document feedback, and consolidate decisions. Video meetings are reserved for key stages such as framing, validation, workshops or other critical phases.

Work is conducted primarily in a **remote and asynchronous setting**. In-person meetings can be arranged depending on the project context.
